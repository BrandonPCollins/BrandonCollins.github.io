Writing

Samples

An Oath Fulfilled

CK3AGOT · Kingsguard Event Chain · Drama

Triggers when a Kingsguard member dies or is stripped of their cloak. The event adapts its tone — from eulogy to disgrace — and scales its prose to reflect the fallen knight's actual in-game skill level, before presenting the king with meaningfully distinct recruitment choices.

"She was a warrior of incredible renown. Those who had the fortune to see her with a blade in hand described her as having fought like the Warrior incarnate. Her steel now rests alongside her, but it will echo in the minds of young warriors across the realm for centuries."

The Throne Denies Her

CK3AGOT · Flavor Event · Dark Comedy

A ruler seated on the Iron Throne is cut by its blades mid-court session — and the smallfolk immediately spin it into an omen of illegitimacy. Plays with the in-world superstition that the Throne itself judges kings, and uses the player's existing court context to make the moment land.

"Suddenly a white heat streaks across my arm, and I recoil in pain... 'She has been rebuked!' calls another. '[Name] is no true ruler!'"

The Teats

CK3AGOT · Flavor Event · Comedy

A lighthearted travel event triggered when riding through the Riverlands with a lover. Shows range — bawdy humor delivered with restraint, player choice that's inconsequential but characterful, and the ability to write comedy that fits an otherwise serious setting.

"This particular tract of land is popularly known as the Teats, for its not-so-subtle resemblance to a woman's bodice. Though tilting your head suddenly they remind you of a pair that you are intimately familiar with."

Vows Rejected

CK3AGOT · Dialogue · Character Voice

A Kingsguard candidate refuses the appointment. Each rejection line is tied to a specific character trait — lust, ambition, hatred, faith — giving players a moment of character revelation rather than a generic refusal.

"My life has far greater purpose ahead of it than standing by your chamberpot while you scutter out your latest suckling goose strips."

Process

Design Thinking

The Kingsguard System: Narrative Serving Mechanics

The Kingsguard in CK3AGOT is a full system with recruitment loops, council integration, succession events, and edge cases like renegade knights and family member reactions. My job was to make sure every mechanical state had a narrative moment attached to it that felt earned rather than procedural.

The vacancy event, for example, branches not just on how a knight left (death vs. disgrace) but on their actual skill level — a legendary warrior gets a genuine eulogy, a poor fit gets quiet acknowledgement. The player reads the quality of their own court back through the prose.

◆

Design principle: The text should reflect the game state, not ignore it. If a Kingsguard was barely adequate, the event should feel different than if you just lost your greatest champion. Branching prose on skill tier was the solution — same event, four different emotional registers.

Writing Comedy Inside a Serious Setting

Events like The Teats and the drunken Kingsguard walk a line that's easy to get wrong — too broad and they break the setting, too restrained and they're not funny. The rule I worked to was that the humor had to be grounded in something real about the world or the characters. The Teats works because the rename mechanic gives the joke a mechanical punchline. The drunken knight works because his behavior is embarrassing to the player's court, not just played for laughs in a vacuum.

Scripting What I Write

Every event I've written on CK3AGOT I've also scripted — handling triggers, effects, scoped variables, mtth weights, and flag logic. Understanding the technical layer has made me a better narrative designer: I know what's cheap to branch and what costs, which shapes how I write choices before I ever open a text file.
